Study Resources

stuff that comes in handy when you're a scholar (WIP)

A scholar needs to have a ton of resources that he/she can refer to for studying or learning. This is especially helpful if he/she is an :autodidact.

Currently, I am starting my senior year of highschool in a CBSE school with an NCERT syllabus. And I do not recommend reading the NCERT books at all, except for the exercises, if you want to actually learn something.

Learners optimise.

I adore these resources that I have listed to improve your knowledge and to have fun while studying.

Last Updated: October 02, 2025

Note: Audio-visual media are marked with bold while textual media in italics

Overall #

  • Khan Academy
  • CrashCourse
  • OpenStax
    • FREE and well written books for a wide variety of subjects, mostly introductory.
  • LibreTexts

Physics #

  • Lectures by Walter Lewin. They will make you love Physics
    • Indeed they will!
    • Esp. for highschool/college level physics
  • Chad's Prep
  • Flipping Physics
    • Cool explanations and visuals
    • Flipped classroom model rocks!
  • Physics Girl
  • The Feynman Lectures: learn from the best.

You also need to find a ton of problem sets!

Chemistry #

  • Chad's Prep (see above)
  • Seema Dhawan Arora Chemistry
    • Great for NCERT chemistry, awesome explanations.
  • ChemistNate
    • Awesome Explanations
  • Prof. Dave Explains
    • Great for mixing basic with high level concepts + practice
  • Tyler DeWitt

Math #

Calculus

  • Paul's Online Notes
    • top-tier math notes + problem sets esp. for Calculus I, II, III.
  • Professor Leonard: amazing math teacher for precalc/calculus and intuition
  • Calculus Made Easy (book by Silvanus P. Thompson)

Olympiad/Higher Math

  • 3Blue1Brown | YT
  • Art of Problem Solving | YT
    • Contests Page
    • Alcumus for grinding
  • Michael Penn
  • Mu Prime Math
  • MODS Infobase for various olympiad math resources
  • Evan Chen; olympiad page
  • AMCTrivial for AMC/AIME problem set grinding

Misc

  • Better Explained: learn based on intuition.
  • ossu/math to study a full undergrad math course at home.
  • blackpenredpen; 100 derivative problems
  • Wumbo References

Comp Sci #

  • Teach Yourself Computer Science
  • ossu/computer-science

Sociology #

(based on my experience of ultralearning a semester worth of sociology.)

  • Introduction to Sociology (3e) - OpenStax’s Free Textbook which reads like a damn novel.
  • Invitation to Sociology: A Humanistic Perspective - Peter L. Berger; amazing, witty and digestible invitation.
  • Crash Course Sociology for the things that you can’t listen from books.

Simulations #

  • PhET

Course Sites #

  • MIT OpenCourseWare
  • MITx
  • edX
  • Coursera
